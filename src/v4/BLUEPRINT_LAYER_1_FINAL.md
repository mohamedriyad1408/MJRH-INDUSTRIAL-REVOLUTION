# MJRH V4 Blueprint — Layer 1: The Constitutional Layer (FINAL - APPROVED)

## 1. Rationale: The Root Trust Layer
Layer 1 is the sovereign foundation of the platform. It defines **Structural Existence** and **Data Ownership Boundaries**. It does not grant rights; it defines entities and their belonging.

## 2. Architectural Principles
- **Single Source of Organizational Truth:** Centralized topology.
- **Organization First:** No object exists without an organizational root.
- **Identity Before Behavior:** Structural presence precedes action.
- **Scope Before Access:** Boundaries are defined before rights are evaluated.
- **Structure Never Knows Business:** Industry-neutral node management.
- **No Business Logic:** Zero conditional business rules in this layer.
- **Immutable Identity:** Stable, versioned organizational identifiers.
- **Explicit Boundaries:** Hard isolation between sovereign entities.

## 3. Strict Ownership (Structural Domain)
- **Legal Identity:** Formal registration of entities.
- **Topology:** The recursive tree of organizational units.
- **Scope:** Definition of data visibility and containment boundaries.
- **Context:** The secure wrapper for all cross-layer operations.

## 4. Exclusion List (Responsibility Boundaries)
Layer 1 MUST NOT contain:
- Authority, Mandates, Permissions, Roles (Moved to L2).
- Workflows, Business Logic, Resource Management, Events, Evidence, or Insight.

## 5. Resilience & Integrity
Layer 1 is the most critical infrastructure component:
- **High Availability:** Geo-distributed.
- **Full Replication:** Real-time data redundancy.
- **Versioning:** Snapshots of organizational changes over time.

## 6. The Constitutional Interface (TrustGate)
- `ResolveContext(ID)`: Identifies the sovereign container.
- `ResolveHierarchy(NodeID)`: Resolves the ancestry and path.
- `ValidateBoundary(A, B)`: Ensures structural relationship integrity.
